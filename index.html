Here's a short summary of Mahi's exploits:

He is the only player to have won the ICC ODI Player of the Year award twice.

The cricketer has also earned title of ‘Lieutenant Colonel’.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni popularly known as M.S.Dhoni was nicknamed captain cool, his level headedness and calm composure in trying situations earned him that nickname.

Dhoni is the only captain to have won three ICC trophies -- T20 World Cup in 2007, ODI World Cup in 2011 and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.

Dhoni captained India when the team rose to the No 1 spot in the Test rankings for the first time in 2009. Dhoni was also the most successful Indian captain with 27 Test wins, eclipsing the legendary Sourav Ganguly's 21 victories before he was overtaken by Virat Kohli, with 36 wins and 14 losses.

Dhoni is the fifth most successful wicket-keeper in Tests with 294 dismissals -- 256 catches and 38 stumpings. He is the third most successful glovesman in ODIs with 444 dismissals in 350 games, including 321 catches and 123 stumpings.

In 2013, Dhoni scored a brilliant 224 against Australia in Chennai. He is also the only Indian wicket-keeper-batsman to score a double-century in Test cricket.

Dhoni last played for India in the 2019 World Cup semi-final against New Zealand in July 2019.
